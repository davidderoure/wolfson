\textbf{Stage} & \textbf{Time} & \textbf{What to demonstrate} \\
\midrule

\textcolor{sparse}{\textbf{Sparse}} &
0:00–1:00 &
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Play quietly and sparingly} — sax mirrors your dynamic level; a whisper-quiet opening draws a tentative response. Contrast with later stages will be dramatic.
  \item \textbf{Establish a short motif (2–3 notes)} — plant the seed early; the system will develop and quote it back in recapitulation.
\end{itemize} \\

\addlinespace[4pt]

\textcolor{building}{\textbf{Building}} &
1:00–2:30 &
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Play D Dorian} (D E F G A B C) — once you have 4+ distinct pitch classes the sax shifts its harmonic language to match. Switch \textit{away} briefly to hear the response change.
  \item \textbf{Repeat a riff} — play the same phrase 3+ times. On the third repeat the sax enters development mode: stronger motivic use, directed contour. The sax starts to \textit{argue with} rather than echo the riff.
  \item \textbf{Let the sax repeat} (\texttt{-\,-sax-riff-prob}) — the reverse: the sax insists on its own phrase while you develop underneath. After two replays it varies the phrase itself. Listen for it evolving its insistence.
\end{itemize} \\

\addlinespace[4pt]

\textcolor{peak}{\textbf{Peak}} &
2:30–3:30 &
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Play loudly and densely, then sparsely} — a busy bass line invites a sparser sax response; a terse two-note phrase invites a rush of notes. Flip between them to show the system reacting.
  \item \textbf{Play a rising phrase, then a falling one} — question-and-answer contour steering. The sax responds to a rising line with a descending resolution and vice versa.
\end{itemize} \\

\addlinespace[4pt]

\textcolor{recap}{\textbf{Recapitulation}} &
3:30–4:30 &
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Play low} — register contrast is strongest here; playing in the low register pushes the sax into the upper register. The spatial separation is most audible.
  \item \textbf{Listen} — the sax quotes lyrical motifs from the sparse and building stages. Pause briefly to let the audience hear the system recalling what was played earlier.
\end{itemize} \\

\addlinespace[4pt]

\textcolor{resolution}{\textbf{Resolution}} &
4:30–5:00 &
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Stop playing} — the sax is designed to close the piece unaccompanied. Put the bass down deliberately and let Wolfson play the final phrase alone. The clearest demonstration of musical agency.
\end{itemize} \\

\textbf{Tips:}\enspace
